Methodology · ZA-DIVERGENT

How It Works

ZA-DIVERGENT is a machine-scored intelligence bulletin covering South African corruption, crime, and governance. Signals are collected from open sources, verified against court records, and organized into active investigations.

{# ── What is this ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
What is ZA-DIVERGENT

ZA-DIVERGENT is the public intelligence bulletin produced by the FORGE OSINT Engine — a local-first, analyst-grade intelligence operating system built for the collection, scoring, and visual analysis of open-source intelligence.

Every signal on this site is sourced from publicly available material: news outlets, government gazettes, court records, satellite data, and social media. Nothing on this site is classified, leaked, or obtained through non-public means.

The bulletin is designed and operated out of South Africa, with a primary focus on domestic and regional open-source intelligence.

{# ── How signals are scored ─────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Gravity Scoring

Every signal is assigned a gravity score between 0.0 and 1.0 by the FORGE analytical pipeline. This score represents editorial weight — how significant the signal is based on source reliability, recency, keyword density, and corroboration with existing intelligence.

The score is displayed as a named significance band:

Score rangeBandMeaning
0.75 – 1.00CriticalMultiple corroborating sources, high urgency, verified actors
0.55 – 0.74HighStrong source, significant subject matter, escalation threshold
0.35 – 0.54SignificantEstablished signal, meets monitoring threshold
0.20 – 0.34NotableSingle-source or developing, worth tracking
0.00 – 0.19RoutineLow-weight background signal
{# ── Evidence Confidence ────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Evidence Confidence (Cases)

Each investigation case has an Evidence Confidence score — the average gravity score across all signals pinned to that case. This reflects the overall evidentiary weight of the investigation:

Score rangeLabelWhat it means
0.75+CorroboratedMultiple independent high-quality sources confirm the investigation's findings
0.55 – 0.74StrongReliable sources with substantial corroboration
0.35 – 0.54EstablishedInvestigation has a solid evidence base from verified sources
0.20 – 0.34DevelopingEarly-stage investigation, limited corroboration so far
Below 0.20PreliminarySparse evidence, mostly single-source signals

{# ── Trust and transparency ─────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Trust and Transparency

All sources are open. Every signal on this site is derived from publicly available material. Source attribution is displayed on every signal card.

Court records provide external authority. When SAFLII case numbers appear (e.g. [2024] ZAGPPHC 441), these are immutable, verifiable identifiers that can be independently checked on saflii.org.

Inclusion is not accusation. An actor appearing in the entity directory reflects relevance to a published investigation, not a legal finding. Analytical conclusions are clearly labelled as assessments.

Corrections are published. When information is found to be incorrect, corrections are noted at the top of the affected article or profile.

{# ── Map markers ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Map Markers

Markers on the signal map represent geo-tagged intelligence. Their visual behaviour communicates two dimensions:

Pulsing markers indicate signals published within the last 48 hours. Fast, bright pulses indicate high gravity (Critical/High significance). Slower pulses indicate moderate gravity. Static markers represent older, digested signals.

Cluster bubbles with numbers appear when multiple signals overlap geographically. Click to zoom in and separate them. The cluster colour indicates density: green (small), amber (medium), red (large cluster).

Dashed lines between markers connect signals from the same investigation case that are geographically separated, showing the spatial extent of an investigation.
